Breaking News

Jamaica records strongest economic growth in a decade as tourism and exports surge

Staff Reporter

April 3, 2026 · 4 min read

The Kingston waterfront, where new commercial developments have contributed to the economic upturn. Photo: Gleaner Archive

The Statistical Institute of Jamaica reported GDP growth of 4.8% for the first quarter of 2026, driven by record visitor arrivals and a 22% increase in agricultural exports. Economists say the figures signal a sustained recovery following years of post-pandemic restructuring.

Tourism arrivals reached 1.2 million in the first quarter alone, surpassing pre-pandemic highs for the first time. The Ministry of Tourism attributed the growth to aggressive marketing campaigns in North America and Europe, as well as improved airlift capacity from major carriers.

"These figures confirm what we have long believed — Jamaica's fundamentals are strong, and the economy is well-positioned for sustained growth over the next decade."

— Minister of Finance, Dr. Nigel Clarke

Agricultural exports, particularly coffee, cocoa, and pimento, saw a 22% year-on-year increase, benefiting from favourable weather conditions and new trade agreements with European markets. The bauxite sector also contributed positively, with production volumes up 14% compared to the same period last year.
