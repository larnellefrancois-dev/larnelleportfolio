Design Brief

The bank needed a typeface that communicated stability, precision, and digital readiness — one that could serve both print collateral and high-density data interfaces without compromise.

Core Requirements
  • Legible at 8px in data tables
  • Distinct numerals — no ambiguity
  • Geometric but warm — not cold
  • Scalable across 5 weights

Phase 02 — Geometric Construction

Every curve is derived from
a mathematical ratio.

O
Glyph O — Circular construction
Construction Principles
  • 01 Stroke terminals cut at precise angles, not rounded
  • 02 Counter shapes derived from the same base circle
  • 03 Optical corrections applied at joints and curves
  • 04 Stem width ratio locked at 1:8 across all weights

Phase 03 — Glyph Set

450+ glyphs covering Latin,
numerals, and symbols.

Numeral Design — Banking Context

0123456789

Tabular figures with fixed-width sidebearings ensure perfect column alignment in financial data tables. Lining numerals only — no old-style figures in the banking context.

Currency & Financial Symbols

$ £ € ¥ ¢ %

All currency symbols are optically balanced to match the cap height and weight of surrounding numerals, preventing visual disruption in financial displays.
